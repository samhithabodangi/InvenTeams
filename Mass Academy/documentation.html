Documentation

Throughout our invention process, we have written and compiled multiple documents for different purposes. Effective documentation has been vital in tracking our progress, facilitating collaboration, and ensuring that our ideas and decisions are clearly recorded and communicated. Below are four of our most important documents that reflect the key milestones and insights gained throughout our development journey. These documents are a part of our final Lemelson-MIT InventTeams submission.

Background

Our background summary highlights the major findings from our literature and market research. By identifying the need for affordable and portable braille displays in the VIP community, we developed a solution and gained feedback from VIPs to create multiple iterations.

Logbook

We have maintained a detailed log of our progress and kept track of important stages during our project timeline. From the brainstorming phase to our device construction, we have documented each step, refining our approach based on the insights gained along the way.

Project Organization

As our project progresses, we hope to invite more people to our team to work towards a shared goal of addressing the problems faced by VIPs. Our project organization document serves to communicate our timeline and how we plan to organize a collaborative team.

Community Engagement

We have connected with different organizations and participated in community outreach events. In doing so, we aim to include VIPs in every step of our device development to ensure that our device has the most effective design tailored to the needs of its users.
